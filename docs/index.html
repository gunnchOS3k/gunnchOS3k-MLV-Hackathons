About gunnchOS3k MLV

gunnchOS3k MLV is a Northwest Indiana-rooted technology education and innovation organization focused on making computing, AI, games, and emerging technology accessible to learners of all backgrounds.

We create beginner-friendly learning experiences that help students, working professionals, and curious community members build practical projects, meet collaborators, and see themselves as future technologists.

Major League Hacking, often called MLH, supports hackathons and hacker communities around the world.

What is a Hackathon?

A hackathon is not about breaking into computers. It is a creative building event.

People form teams, choose an idea, use technology or design tools, build a prototype, and present what they made. A “hack” can be an app, website, game, AI assistant, data story, community resource, pitch deck, prototype, or demo video.

You can build without coding Many strong projects use Google Docs, Slides, Sheets, Canva, recorded demo videos, storyboards, or AI-assisted planning. Coding is welcome — but it is not required.

Zero-to-Hero Build Sprint Hyperbolic Time Machine Experience

Walk In Curious. Walk Out Portfolio Ready.

Participants walk in as curious learners and leave as documented builders.

Proof of work, not just participation

This event is designed as a zero-to-hero build sprint. Participants are not expected to arrive as experts. They are expected to arrive with curiosity, courage, and willingness to try. Through rapid problem solving, design thinking, prototyping, documentation, and presentation, participants will leave with more than a memory — they will leave with proof of work.

These outcomes describe skills and artifacts you can earn through building. They do not guarantee jobs, prizes, scholarships, or professional status.

The Proof of Work Packet

Every participant or team should leave with a Proof of Work Packet. It does not need to be perfect. It needs to exist.

Minimum packet

Beginner-friendly — this path is enough.

  • Project title
  • Problem statement
  • Who it helps
  • What was built
  • Tools used
  • Screenshots or photos
  • Demo video or live demo
  • Slides
  • README or one-page explanation
  • What we learned
  • What we would build next

Fully loaded packet

Aspirational — grow into this over time.

  • Public GitHub repo or portfolio page
  • GitHub Pages or personal website project page
  • PowerPoint / Google Slides pitch deck
  • Technical README
  • LaTeX-style technical report (optional)
  • Reproducibility checklist
  • Demo video
  • Screenshots / GIFs
  • Asset and license credits
  • Resume / LinkedIn bullet
  • Reflection: “Before this event, I thought I could not… Now I can…”

Inclusive path A phone-only beginner can still complete the ladder. A clear idea, a working prototype, a short video, and strong documentation can be a real portfolio artifact.

Pressure With Guardrails

This is meant to be a positive pressure-cooker experience, not burnout or humiliation. The goal is to stretch participants with time constraints, teamwork, demos, and public explanation — while still providing mentorship, beginner tracks, phone-first tools, youth safety rules, and encouragement.

The pressure is the mold, but support is the safety rail.

Phone-First Promise

We are designing this hackathon so a person with only a phone can still participate and submit a project.

Laptops, tablets, and Chromebooks are welcome, but they are not required to be part of the event.

Project types may include:

  • Paper sketch photographed and explained

Parents & Guardians

We want families to feel informed and comfortable. Consent forms and youth safety policies will be shared before registration opens.

  • What students will do: Learn, collaborate, build a project, present a demo, and follow event rules in a supervised community setting.
  • How adults can support: Help with transportation, encouragement, guardian consent, and age-appropriate tool setup at home.
  • Consent: Release and consent forms will be required for minors once finalized.
  • Photos and media: Choices will be explained before the event; photography volunteers only after release forms are in place.
  • Code of Conduct: Applies to all participants, volunteers, and mentors.
  • Mentoring safety: No one-on-one private adult/minor mentoring.
  • Privacy in projects: Participants should not share passwords, home addresses, private school IDs, medical details, or other sensitive personal information in projects.
