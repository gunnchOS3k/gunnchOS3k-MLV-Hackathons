# What is a Hackathon?

A hackathon is **not** about breaking into computers or doing anything illegal. It is a **creative building event** where people come together to learn, collaborate, and make something new in a short amount of time.

## What happens at a hackathon?

1. **People arrive** — beginners and experienced builders, often in teams or ready to form teams  
2. **Teams choose an idea** — something useful, fun, creative, or community-focused  
3. **Teams build a prototype** — using code, design tools, documents, slides, games, AI helpers, or video  
4. **Teams present** — a short demo so judges and the community can understand what they made  

## What can you build?

A “hack” (project) can be many things:

- Mobile-friendly web app (e.g., Replit)  
- Website or landing page  
- Game or interactive story  
- AI-assisted helper or chatbot concept  
- Google Doc plan or Google Slides pitch  
- Google Sheets tracker, budget, or data story  
- Canva mockup or visual prototype  
- Demo video recorded on your phone  
- Paper sketch plus explanation  

## You can build without coding

Strong projects often use:

- **Google Docs** — plan and describe your idea  
- **Google Slides** — pitch deck  
- **Google Sheets** — data, schedules, budgets  
- **Gemini** — brainstorm and explain your project  
- **Canva** — visual design  
- **Camera** — photos and demo video  

Coding is welcome. It is **not required**.
