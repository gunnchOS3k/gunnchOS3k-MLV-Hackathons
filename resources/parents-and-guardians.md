# Parents and Guardians Guide

**Planned Summer 2026 hack days · Merrillville pilot — venue confirmation pending**

Thank you for supporting a young person’s interest in technology, creativity, and community building. This guide explains what to expect and how to help — with honest notes about what is still being finalized.

---

## What is this event?

A **beginner-friendly, phone-first hackathon** (Hack Day) where participants learn, collaborate, build a project, and present a short demo. It is **not** about hacking into systems illegally.

**Core message for families:** Your child does **not** need a thousand-dollar laptop. A phone, curiosity, and willingness to learn can be enough.

---

## What will students do?

- Meet other students and community members  
- Form or join a team  
- Choose a project idea (with mentor support)  
- Build using phone-friendly tools (Docs, Slides, Sheets, Replit, video, design tools, etc.)  
- Present a demo to judges and the community  
- Follow the **Code of Conduct** and venue rules  

---

## How can adults support?

- Encourage curiosity and teamwork  
- Help set up a **Google account** and Bare Bones Toolkit if needed  
- Ensure reliable transportation once date and venue are confirmed  
- Complete **guardian consent forms** when they are released (required for minors)  
- Review photo/media choices when options are explained  
- Remind students not to share sensitive personal data in projects  

---

## Minors and consent

- Minors are **planned** to participate with **guardian consent** and event safety rules  
- **Consent and release forms** will be required once finalized  
- **Youth safety policies** will be shared **before registration opens**  
- **Parent attendance details** will be included in the youth safety plan  

---

## Safety practices

- **Code of Conduct** applies to all participants, volunteers, and mentors  
- **No one-on-one private** adult/minor mentoring  
- **Harassment is not tolerated** — report concerns to organizers  
- **Photography/media volunteers** only after release forms are in place  
- Students should **not share** passwords, home addresses, private school IDs, medical details, or similar sensitive information in projects  

See [Code of Conduct Summary](code-of-conduct-summary.md) and the [MLH Code of Conduct](https://github.com/MLH/mlh-policies/blob/main/code-of-conduct.md).
