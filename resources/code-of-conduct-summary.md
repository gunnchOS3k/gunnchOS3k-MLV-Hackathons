# Code of Conduct Summary

Everyone participating in **gunnchOS3k MLV** planned hack days — attendees, volunteers, mentors, judges, and guests — is expected to follow this summary and the full **[MLH Code of Conduct](https://github.com/MLH/mlh-policies/blob/main/code-of-conduct.md)**.

---

## Our community values

- **Respect** — Listen, encourage, and treat others with dignity  
- **Inclusion** — Welcome beginners, phone-only participants, and diverse backgrounds  
- **Safety** — Especially important when minors are present  
- **Learning** — Ask questions, share knowledge, celebrate effort  

---

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful in words and actions  
- Help create a welcoming environment for first-time hackers  
- Ask for help when you need it  
- Follow venue rules once the venue is confirmed  
- Keep projects appropriate for a **community event with minors**  
- Report concerns to **event organizers** promptly  

---

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, discrimination, or intimidation of any kind  
- Sexual language or imagery in community spaces  
- Disruptive behavior that ruins others’ learning experience  
- Sharing others’ private information without permission  
- One-on-one private adult/minor mentoring (not allowed)  

---

## Project safety

Projects should not promote harm, illegal activity, hate, or explicit content unsuitable for a youth-inclusive community event.

Do not include in projects:

- Passwords or API secrets  
- Home addresses or private contact info  
- Medical or sensitive personal data about real individuals without consent  

---

## Reporting

If you experience or witness a concern:

1. Find an **event organizer** or volunteer wearing staff identification (details at check-in)  
2. Describe what happened — you will be taken seriously  
3. Organizers will follow up per MLH and local policies  

---

## Consequences

Organizers may warn, remove, or ban participants who violate the Code of Conduct, in coordination with MLH policies and venue requirements.
